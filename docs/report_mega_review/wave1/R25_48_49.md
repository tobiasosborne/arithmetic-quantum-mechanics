## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/49_regular_functions_affine_line_weyl_labels.tex:153`
  Issue:
  The Chinese-remainder independence claim is central to this shard but is not
  source-anchored or locally proved at the repository's required evidence
  standard.
  Evidence:
  The source-anchor paragraph lists Stacks affine-scheme/global-section,
  spectrum/open/residue-field locators, AQM-28, and AQM-36
  (`report/sections/49_regular_functions_affine_line_weyl_labels.tex:11`), but
  it does not list a Chinese remainder theorem locator. Lemma L3 uses the
  pairwise-coprime product divisibility step
  (`report/sections/49_regular_functions_affine_line_weyl_labels.tex:157`), and
  Theorem T2 invokes "The Chinese remainder theorem" to get the surjection
  `R -> product_i R/(pi_i)`
  (`report/sections/49_regular_functions_affine_line_weyl_labels.tex:177`).
  Under `AGENTS.md` Law 1, this is not finished as a checked/source-local
  mathematical claim.
  Suggested action:
  Add a precise local source locator for the Chinese remainder theorem, or add
  a short local Bezout/idempotent derivation proving both the product-divisibility
  step in L3 and the residue-surjectivity step in T2.

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/49_regular_functions_affine_line_weyl_labels.tex:188`
  Issue:
  The thickened Artin-ring comparison is stated over `k = F_Q`, but the cited
  local thickened Weyl layer is F3-specific.
  Evidence:
  AQM-49 sets `k = F_Q` at
  `report/sections/49_regular_functions_affine_line_weyl_labels.tex:52`, then
  says AQM-36 quantizes the thickened layer by
  `E_h^thick = direct_sum_i A_i^2` and
  `H_h^thick = tensor_i ell^2(A_i)`
  (`report/sections/49_regular_functions_affine_line_weyl_labels.tex:195`), and
  uses the cardinality `Q^(e_i deg pi_i)`
  (`report/sections/49_regular_functions_affine_line_weyl_labels.tex:211`).
  But AQM-36 defines the local Artin layer for
  `k = F_3`, `A = k[t]/(pi^e)` and a top-coefficient character into `F_3`
  (`report/sections/36_nilpotent_thickening_weyl_fields.tex:30` and
  `report/sections/36_nilpotent_thickening_weyl_fields.tex:84`). Convention
  `(ab)` is likewise written for `F_3[t]`, not arbitrary `F_Q[x]`
  (`CONVENTIONS.md:879`). Thus the reduced-versus-thickened distinction is
  locally supported for F3, but the general finite-field thickened Weyl claim is
  not yet conventioned or sourced.
  Suggested action:
  Either restrict the AQM-49 thickened Weyl comparison to `k = F_3`, or add a
  general finite-field Artin/Frobenius-ring convention and source/local proof
  for the generating character before using `W_h^thick` over arbitrary `F_Q`.

## Cross-Shard Risks

AQM-48 is consistent with AQM-28, AQM-29, AQM-45, AQM-46, and conventions `(y)`,
`(z)`, and `(aj)`: the rational three-site model is treated as a finite-support
subalgebra, the generic point is topological but not a finite-residue qudit, and
the zero-reference Hilbert sector is preserved because residue-field
relabelings send `0` to `0`.

The main cross-shard risk is terminology drift in later regular-function and
QSA shards: "thickened Weyl operator" should not be imported over arbitrary
finite fields merely from the reduced residue-field statement unless the
Artin-ring character convention has been generalized beyond the F3 layer.

## Suggested Actions

1. Add a local Chinese-remainder source or proof to AQM-49 before relying on
   finite closed-point independence.
2. Qualify AQM-49's thickened comparison as F3-local, or generalize the
   Artin-ring Weyl convention with explicit finite-field character data.
3. Keep AQM-48 unchanged unless later reviewers find a C*-completion issue; its
   present claims are algebraic and match the registered incomplete-tensor
   convention.
