## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/17_projective_line_stalks.tex:54`
  Issue:
  The displayed description of \(M_{(\mathfrak p)}\) uses the wrong ambient
  object for denominators. It says homogeneous fractions have
  \(a,b\in M\), \(\deg a=\deg b\), and \(b\notin\mathfrak p\). For a graded
  \(S\)-module \(M\), the denominator should be a homogeneous element of
  \(S\), not of \(M\). As written, the formula is not meaningful for a general
  module \(M\), since \(\mathfrak p\subset S\) does not contain or exclude
  elements of \(M\) in that way.
  Evidence:
  The shard invokes the Proj stalk formula for a graded `S`-module at
  `report/sections/17_projective_line_stalks.tex:49-58`. The registered source
  manifest points to Stacks Proj stalk formula lines in
  `references/algebraic_geometry/SOURCES.md:19`, and the local convention
  separately records the same Proj/sheaf setup for \(O(d)\) sections in
  `CONVENTIONS.md:424-436`.
  Suggested action:
  Rewrite the fraction sentence as: \(m/f\), where \(m\in M\) and
  \(f\in S\) are homogeneous, \(f\notin\mathfrak p\), and their degrees match
  after the grading shift. Then keep the specialized \(\mathcal O(d)\) stalk
  computations as consequences.
