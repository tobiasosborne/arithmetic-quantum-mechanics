## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/18_stalks_as_field_germs.tex:127`
  Issue: The text says the germ-to-value maps are "not extra choices", but the
  displayed scalar values in \(V\) also use the fixed local frame / representative
  evaluation convention for \(\mathcal O(d)\).
  Evidence: The quotient \(\mathcal E_{d,P}\to\mathcal E_d(P)\) is canonical,
  but identifying the line-bundle fiber with a scalar and then with
  \(V=\mathbb F_3^2\) depends on the chosen frames \(e_X^{(d)}\), \(e_Y^{(d)}\)
  and the fixed representative evaluation convention.  `CONVENTIONS.md:438`
  says monomials are evaluated on chosen representatives and this is not a
  claim that \(\mathcal O(d)\) sections are global scalar functions;
  `CONVENTIONS.md:457` records the chosen frames; `CONVENTIONS.md:463` says the
  fiber is represented as \(V\) after the chosen local frame is reduced.  The
  same caveat appears in `report/sections/15_projective_sheaf_fields_definitions.tex:185`
  where evaluations of nontrivial line-bundle sections require chosen
  trivializations or representatives.
  Suggested action: Split the sentence: the residue quotient is canonical, but
  the scalar table and the identification \(\mathcal E_d(P)\cong V\) use the
  already-declared projective-line frame/evaluation convention.

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/19_canonical_boson_fermion_field_comparison.tex:116`
  Issue: The theorem statement says the real-target AQM-14 recipe recovers the
  standard finite bosonic canonical field, but the hypotheses needed for the
  Stone--von Neumann step are not part of D4/T1.
  Evidence: D4 at lines 35 and 72--80 only asks for a Weyl-form CCR
  representation.  The Stone--von Neumann source cited in the proof requires a
  regular representation and gives the Schrodinger representation up to
  multiplicity; irreducibility is the one-copy case
  (`references/canonical_fields/derezinski_math_ph_0511030_source/derezinski.tex:1261`).
  The shard's Lemma L2 says this correctly at lines 100--114, but T1 at
  lines 116--128 reads as if the field-label recipe alone determines the
  standard representation.
  Suggested action: Add "regular irreducible" to D4/T1 or restate T1 as:
  after choosing the regular irreducible Weyl representation of
  \((E_B,\Omega_B)\), Stone--von Neumann identifies it with the Schrodinger
  representation; regular non-irreducible representations are Schrodinger
  copies up to multiplicity.

## Cross-Shard Risks

The same convention boundary recurs across `AQM-15`--`AQM-18`: projective-line
stalk quotients are canonical, but scalar evaluations of \(\mathcal O(d)\)
sections use a chosen trivialization/representative convention.  Later reviewers
should watch for statements that treat the resulting scalar rows as canonical
line-bundle evaluations without naming that convention.

The boson/fermion layer is mostly source-backed, but the phrase "recovers the
standard finite bosonic field" should consistently mean the regular irreducible
finite-mode CCR representation.  Without that qualifier, later shards could
silently use Stone--von Neumann uniqueness outside its regularity and
irreducibility hypotheses.

## Suggested Actions

Update `AQM-18` to separate canonical residue quotient maps from chosen scalar
frame/evaluation identifications.

Update `AQM-19` to put the regular irreducible CCR representation in the
theorem hypothesis, add the missing Cahill--Glauber source anchors, and fix the
`AQM-20`/`AQM-21` cross-reference wording.
