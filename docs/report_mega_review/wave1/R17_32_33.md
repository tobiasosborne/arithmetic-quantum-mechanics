## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/32_weyl_heisenberg_representations_for_kt.tex:40`
  Issue: The Stone-von Neumann boundary is stated too absolutely: the local Bekka source does not only give an obstruction for countably infinite discrete rings; it immediately records a weaker approximate-equivalence Stone-von Neumann theorem that appears applicable to \(K=k(t)\).
  Evidence: AQM-32 lines 40-41 say Stone-von Neumann uniqueness requires the full LCA dual or a local/adelic completion, and AQM-33 lines 159-162 repeat that uniqueness belongs to the full discrete-dual or completed objects. The cited Bekka obstruction is real for the strong self-duality theorem: `references/heisenberg_weil/bekka_2502_00387_source/CCR-GeneralRings-v5.tex:384-387` says countably infinite discrete rings cannot satisfy the isomorphism condition and that theorem fails. But the same source continues at lines 388-423 with a weaker Stone-von Neumann theorem: for a second-countable locally compact ring and a character satisfying symmetry plus the faithfulness condition, infinite amplifications are approximately equivalent to the Schrödinger pair. Since \(k(t)\) is a commutative field and \(\lambda_K\neq0\), the faithfulness condition has no nonzero two-sided ideal in the kernel. The shards do not mention this weaker theorem or explain why it is outside the intended boundary.
  Suggested action: Revise T2/conclusion language to distinguish strong irreducible/direct-sum LCA uniqueness from Bekka's weaker approximate-equivalence theorem for countably infinite discrete rings. Either cite and classify the weaker theorem as out of scope, or add it as a fourth boundary case for the discrete \(k(t)\) ring.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/33_full_dual_local_models_for_kt.tex:74`
  Issue: The density derivation uses an annihilator-of-closure fact from Pontryagin duality, but the shard's source anchors omit the local lines that support this step.
  Evidence: Lines 74-77 conclude that \(\Gamma_K^\perp=\{0\}\) implies \(\overline{\Gamma_K}=\widehat{K_{\rm disc}}\). This is correct, but it is not derived in the shard. The status anchors cite Prasad lines 95-107 and Beny--Crann--Lee--Park--Youn lines 461-473; the local source line that actually defines annihilators for closed subgroups is `references/heisenberg_weil/beny_crann_lee_park_youn_2204_08162_source/main.tex:323`. Without that anchor, the density proof relies on an uncited harmonic-analysis fact.
  Suggested action: Add a precise citation to Beny--Crann--Lee--Park--Youn lines 323-325, or insert a finite-coordinate proof specialized to the discrete \(\mathbb F_p\)-vector space \(K\).

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/33_full_dual_local_models_for_kt.tex:83`
  Issue: The local-field and adelic paragraphs make substantive source-backed claims but cite only the broad source manifest, not precise PDF theorem/page locators.
  Evidence: Lines 83-97 say a nontrivial continuous additive character identifies \(K_\infty\) with its Pontryagin dual and that a residue character from a differential gives the standard self-dual adelic setting. The shard's source anchors at lines 19-21 point only to `references/heisenberg_weil/SOURCES.md` from Poonen and Kudla. The manifest records Poonen Theorem 4.4 for local self-duality and Section 5 for adeles at `references/heisenberg_weil/SOURCES.md:268-272`, and Kudla Theorem 1.1 at lines 284-286. The report rule asks claims to cite local path plus page/theorem/equation/section; this paragraph needs those locators in the shard itself, especially because the source is PDF rather than line-numbered TeX.
  Suggested action: Add exact Poonen theorem/page or section/page citations for local self-duality, self-dual Haar measure, and adelic residue characters, plus Kudla Chapter I, Theorem 1.1 if the local-field Heisenberg uniqueness statement is intended here. If the differential/residue character is only a future convention, mark it as a pending convention rather than a settled construction.

## Cross-Shard Risks

The algebraic \(k(t)\) construction in AQM-32 is internally coherent under `CONVENTIONS.md` `(z)`: the coefficient character is fixed, the \(\chi_K(ab)\) pairing is nondegenerate by the displayed argument, and the commutant proof establishes irreducibility for the concrete discrete algebraic group. The \(k=\mathbb F_3\) examples in AQM-33 lines 113-149 also check out against the coefficient rule.

The main risk is terminology drift around "Stone-von Neumann." AQM-31, AQM-32, and AQM-33 correctly reject the strong self-dual locally compact ring theorem for bare countable discrete \(k(t)\), but they can be read as rejecting every sourced discrete-ring uniqueness analogue. Bekka's weaker approximate-equivalence theorem should be named so later shards do not rediscover it as a contradiction.

## Suggested Actions

Clarify the Stone-von Neumann boundary first: strong full-dual/local/adelic uniqueness versus Bekka approximate equivalence for countably infinite discrete rings. Then add the missing precise anchors for the full-dual density argument and the local/adelic PDF-backed claims.
