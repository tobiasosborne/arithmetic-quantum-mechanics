Focus areas checked: presheaf/cosheaf terminology, the failure of ordinary

## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/27_state_presheaf_quasilocal_algebra.tex:188`
  Issue: The overlapping-marginal counterexample is mathematically right, but
  the positivity/support step is not justified as written.
  Evidence: Lines 188-202 argue that
  \(\operatorname{Tr}(((1-P_{AB})\otimes1_C)\theta_{ABC})=0\) forces
  \(\theta_{ABC}\) to be supported on
  \(\operatorname{ran}(P_{AB})\otimes\mathbb C^2_C\), and then concludes
  \(\theta_{ABC}=P_{AB}\otimes\tau_C\). The operator
  \(((1-P_{AB})\otimes1_C)\theta_{ABC}\) is not itself visibly positive, so the
  sentence "a positive operator with zero trace is zero on that positive part"
  skips the needed projection argument. The standard repair is to set
  \(Q=(1-P_{AB})\otimes1_C\), use
  \(\operatorname{Tr}(Q\theta Q)=\operatorname{Tr}(Q\theta)=0\), conclude
  \(Q\theta Q=0\), and then use positivity to get \(Q\theta=\theta Q=0\).
  Since \(P_{AB}\) is rank one, this gives
  \(\theta=P_{AB}\otimes\tau_C\).
  Suggested action: Expand the proof at lines 188-202 with the \(Q\theta Q\)
  argument, so the no-gluing theorem does not rely on an implicit positivity
  lemma.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/27_state_presheaf_quasilocal_algebra.tex:169`
  Issue: The three-qubit marginal example depends on
  \(\operatorname{Spec}(\mathbb F_2\times\mathbb F_2\times\mathbb F_2)\) being a
  discrete three-point spectrum with one qubit per point, but this local
  identification is asserted rather than sourced or derived.
  Evidence: Lines 169-175 introduce a discrete three-point finite spectrum and
  give the product ring as an example. The source anchors for AQM-27 cover
  density matrices and partial trace, not spectra of product rings. The earlier
  finite-spectrum shards prove the two-point \(\mathbb Z/6\mathbb Z\) case, but
  this specific three-factor field spectrum is not locally derived in AQM-27
  before it is used as the open cover for \(AB\), \(BC\), and \(B\).
  Suggested action: Add a short derivation or citation near line 169: the three
  primitive idempotent factors give three clopen points with residue field
  \(\mathbb F_2\), hence three qubit tensor factors under the residue-qudit
  convention.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/26_presheaves_cosheaves_observable_nets.tex:122`
  Issue: The Weyl-label gluing result is proved only for finite covers, while
  the surrounding definitions define cosheaves using arbitrary open covers.
  Evidence: Lines 91-108 give the ordinary cover coequalizer definition of a
  cosheaf. Lines 122-142 then prove Lemma L1 only for finite covers. The
  conclusion is mostly careful, calling \(E\) "cosheaf-like" at lines 270-272,
  but the shard summary at lines 3-5 and theorem dictionary at lines 240-260
  can be read as promoting label gluing to the general cosheaf layer. This is
  harmless for the finite two-point examples, but it is a cross-shard risk once
  AQM-28 moves to affine spectra with infinitely many closed points.
  Suggested action: Either strengthen Lemma L1 to arbitrary covers for the
  finite-support direct-sum label assignment, or keep the statement explicitly
  as "finite-cover cosheaf gluing" wherever it is used as evidence for later
  Weyl-label nets.

- Severity: Question
  Location: `report/sections/26_presheaves_cosheaves_observable_nets.tex:200`
  Issue: The phrase "coproduct of unital `*`-algebras amalgamated over the
  common unit" is not locally sourced as a category-specific fact.
  Evidence: The Curry source anchors at lines 20-28 support colimits and
  coproducts abstractly, and the proof at lines 206-236 gives a good local
  universal-property counterexample showing that \(M_2\otimes M_3\) is not the
  unital `*`-algebra coproduct. What is not sourced is the stronger
  identification of that coproduct with an amalgamated free product. The report
  does not need the free-product model to prove the failure.
  Suggested action: Either add a local source for the unital `*`-algebra free
  product statement, or rephrase line 200-205 to say only that the cosheaf
  coequalizer would have the coproduct universal property in unital
  `*`-algebras.

## Cross-Shard Risks

AQM-26 and AQM-27 agree with `CONVENTIONS.md` entries `(w)` and `(x)` on the
main directionality: observable algebras are covariant open-local nets, states
are contravariant by restriction, and tensor-local additivity is not ordinary
unital `*`-algebra coproduct gluing.

The main risk is terminology hardening. "Cosheaf-like label layer" is safe;
"the label cosheaf" should remain tied to the exact cover class and the
finite-support closed-point convention used later. Similarly, the state
presheaf failure examples are good finite counterexamples, but their local
finite-spectrum inputs should be derived in the shard so later descent
discussion does not inherit an unproved product-spectrum convention.

## Suggested Actions

Patch the AQM-27 proof of Theorem T3 with the explicit projection-support
argument, add a one-sentence derivation of the three-point product-field
spectrum, and either strengthen or narrow AQM-26's finite-cover label-cosheaf
claim. The unital `*`-algebra cosheaf failure argument is otherwise sound; it
only needs the optional free-product wording sourced or removed.
