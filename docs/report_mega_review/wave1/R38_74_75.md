## Findings

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/74_quantum_system_association_single_particle_reduction.tex:106`
  Issue:
  The inverse of \(R_G\) is described as "the projection from the degree-one distributive expansion onto the singleton summands", but \(R_G\) has codomain \(\mathcal V_1(G_X)\), which is already the degree-one singleton-summand carrier after the AQM-70 identification.
  Evidence:
  AQM-74 defines \(R_G:K_{\oplus}(X)\to\mathcal V_1(G_X)\) at lines 90--104. AQM-70 defines the site-option sector by \(\mathcal V_n(G_X)\cong\bigoplus_{|S|=n}\bigotimes_{x\in S}K_x\), so for \(n=1\) the domain of the inverse is already the singleton direct sum, not the full distributive expansion. A projection from the full expansion would instead have domain \(\mathcal V(G_X)\).
  Suggested action:
  Replace the inverse sentence with an explicit inverse on singleton summands, e.g. send the singleton tensor in the \(x\)-summand to \(j_x(v)\), or state that the full-carrier projection is first restricted to the degree-one sector.

## Cross-Shard Risks

AQM-75 establishes a useful discipline for later shards, but it is easy for AQM-76 onward to say "points" or "finite-residue points" without naming which row of the AQM-75 table is active. Later reviewers should check that downstream finite Weyl/Hilbert claims explicitly name underlying, rational, closed, generic, or all-scheme point choices.

AQM-74 correctly keeps field-label direct sums, Hilbert tensor products, CCR/CAR algebras, statistics, phases, and Gram choices separate. The residual risk is mostly wording drift: summaries or later synthesis tables could overread the carrier-level maps as equivalences of observable algebras or Fock/field structures.
