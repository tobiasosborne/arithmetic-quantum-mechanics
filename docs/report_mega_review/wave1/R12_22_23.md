## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/22_prime_field_arithmetic_fields_qudit_paulis.tex:145`
  Issue: The shard's Weyl commutator sign is internally consistent with the repository convention, but it is the opposite exponent from the cited source equations and the conversion is not stated.
  Evidence: Lines 145-152 state \(W(e)W(f)=\chi(\Omega(e,f))W(f)W(e)\) with \(\Omega((x,z),(x',z'))=zx'-xz'\). The local convention in `CONVENTIONS.md:563-570` makes the same choice, so the derivation is locally coherent. However, the source locators cited at lines 14-23 use the opposite exponent under the same \((X,Z)\) label order: Ashikhmin--Knill `references/symplectic_qecc/ashikhmin_knill_0005008_source/n9.tex:265-268` gives the product exponent \(-jk\) and commutator exponent \(il-jk\); Gottesman `references/symplectic_qecc/gottesman_9705052_source/Thesis.tex:1677-1680` gives \(ad-bc\); Bombin--Martin--Delgado `references/toric_code/bombin_martin_delgado_0605094_source/HomologicalErrorCorrection6.tex:1247-1254` uses the matrix giving \(xz'-zx'\). The shard's exponent is \(zx'-xz'\), i.e. the negative of these source formulas if \(x,z\) are read as the cited \(X,Z\) exponents. This becomes misleading at lines 188-190 and 203-206, which call the group the standard source-convention Pauli group without naming the sign/shift conversion.
  Suggested action: Add an explicit convention bridge near the source anchors or Lemma L3: either say the repo uses the inverse commutator/shift convention relative to the cited \(T,C,X\) formulas, or relabel \(Z\mapsto Z^{-1}\) / reverse the translation convention before claiming agreement with the cited source convention.

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/23_spec_z6_residue_qudit_factorisation.tex:147`
  Issue: The mixed residue Weyl law inherits the same unstated source-sign conversion, now across both the \(\mathbb F_2\) and \(\mathbb F_3\) factors.
  Evidence: Lines 147-159 define \(c_R(e,f)=(-1)^{z_2x'_2}\exp(2\pi i z_3x'_3/3)\) and the commutator bicharacter from \(zx'-xz'\). This matches `CONVENTIONS.md:593-596`, but the shard's source anchor at lines 21-27 points readers back to Ashikhmin--Knill and Bombin--Martin--Delgado, whose displayed commutator exponents are \(xz'-zx'\) under the usual \((X,Z)\) order. Because AQM-23 is the base reference for residue-qudit tensor factors, the missing sign bridge can propagate into later finite-spectrum Weyl-net shards.
  Suggested action: Cross-reference the same sign-conversion sentence requested for AQM-22, or define the local \(W_i\) operators explicitly enough to show why this shard intentionally uses the repository's opposite commutator convention.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/23_spec_z6_residue_qudit_factorisation.tex:141`
  Issue: The \(\mathbb F_2\) factor is treated as a local shift-phase Weyl system, but the shard does not carry forward AQM-22's fourth-root phase caveat for conventional qubit Paulis.
  Evidence: AQM-22 records at `report/sections/22_prime_field_arithmetic_fields_qudit_paulis.tex:249-265` that the unphased \(p=2\) Weyl representatives have the same projective labels but the conventional Hermitian qubit Pauli group uses \(\mu_4\), e.g. \(Y=iXZ\). AQM-23's span result in lines 171-187 is unaffected, because \(X,Z,XZ\) still form an operator basis up to scalar, and the tensor-product conclusion in lines 191-217 is also unaffected. The risk is wording: "usual local shift-phase Weyl operator" and "qubit tensor a qutrit" can be read as importing the full conventional qubit Pauli group, while the proof uses unphased representatives.
  Suggested action: Add one sentence before Lemma L4 or in the conclusion: the \(\mathbb F_2\) site uses the unphased projective Weyl representatives of AQM-22; adjoining the usual \(\mu_4\) phases changes representatives, not the label group, operator span, or Hilbert tensor-product conclusion.

## Cross-Shard Risks

The `Spec(F_p)` versus underlying-set distinction is handled correctly in AQM-22 and matches `CONVENTIONS.md` `(t)`: `Spec(F_p)` gives one site, while the underlying set of `F_p` gives \(p\) sites via all maps. The `Spec(Z/6Z)` calculation and the direct-sum-label versus tensor-product-Hilbert distinction are also locally correct and match `CONVENTIONS.md` `(u)`.

The main cross-shard risk is phase-sign provenance. Later residue-Weyl, open-net, dynamics, and stabilizer-selection shards cite AQM-22/AQM-23 as anchors. If they compare directly against Ashikhmin--Knill, Gottesman, Bombin--Martin--Delgado, or half-Weyl/Gaussian conventions without an explicit sign bridge, phase-sensitive formulas can silently flip.

## Suggested Actions

Fix the source-convention bridge once in AQM-22 and point AQM-23 to it. Then add the short \(\mathbb F_2\) phase caveat in AQM-23 so "qubit tensor qutrit" is clearly a statement about projective Weyl labels, operator spans, and Hilbert tensor factors, not a silent choice of Hermitian Pauli representatives.
