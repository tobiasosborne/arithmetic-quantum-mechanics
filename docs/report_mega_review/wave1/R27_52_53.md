## Findings

- Severity: Question
  Location: `report/sections/52_f3_regular_function_symmetry_line_by_line.tex:189`
  Issue:
  The repeated-factor section states the transported thickened Artin rings and
  dimension preservation, but it does not restate the thickened Weyl-character
  caveat from the parent convention.
  Evidence:
  Lines 189-195 identify the source and target Artin rings and say
  `F -> F(x-1)` gives ring isomorphisms, so the thickened dimension remains
  `3^8`. That ring/dimension statement is consistent with AQM-51 and
  `CONVENTIONS.md:1503-1514`. However the same convention warns that thickened
  Weyl covariance additionally requires transporting the generating character
  or supplying a dual momentum-label correction; reduced residue covariance does
  not prove default top-coefficient Artin character preservation.
  Suggested action:
  Add one sentence after the ring-isomorphism statement: this is only
  Artin-ring support and dimension transport, not a claim of thickened Weyl
  covariance for independently chosen default top-coefficient characters.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/53_minimal_fermion_geometry_catalogue.tex:15`
  Issue:
  Several source anchors are routed only through `SOURCES.md` manifests instead
  of giving the local source line locators in the shard itself.
  Evidence:
  Lines 15-22 cite `references/canonical_fields/SOURCES.md`,
  `references/supergeometry/SOURCES.md`, `references/spin_geometry/SOURCES.md`,
  and `references/log_geometry/SOURCES.md`. Those manifests do contain useful
  locators, but the shard's own status block gives exact line ranges only for
  the Stacks Kähler-differentials source at lines 17-19. The body then makes
  source-sensitive claims about CAR modes, reduced superpoints, Clifford
  quantization of odd variables, spin square roots, and logarithmic forms.
  Suggested action:
  Inline the relevant manifest locators in the status block, matching the style
  already used for the Stacks Kähler-differentials citation. For example, add
  the Derezinski CAR lines, Witten odd-coordinate/reduced-space/parity-reversal
  lines, Atiyah square-root spin lines, and Stacks log-pole lines already
  recorded in the manifests.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/53_minimal_fermion_geometry_catalogue.tex:194`
  Issue:
  The spin/log-spin source boundary is easy to overread as sourced analytic spin
  theory over finite fields.
  Evidence:
  Lines 181-195 define a log-spin datum over a field `k`, then say Atiyah's
  source supports the square-root spin dictionary over compact complex curves
  and that the logarithmic variant is local. This is probably intended as a
  convention-backed catalogue definition, and it matches `CONVENTIONS.md:1561-1571`.
  Still, the finite-field example in lines 255-267 is a concrete `F_3`
  one-mode row, so the shard should make explicit that Atiyah is not being used
  as a finite-field log-spin theorem source.
  Suggested action:
  Add a sentence before Lemma L5 or in its proof: the `F_3` log-spin row is a
  local definition plus the checked trivialization
  `Omega^1_{P^1}(log {0,infty}) ~= O`, not an imported analytic spin-structure
  theorem over finite fields.

## Cross-Shard Risks

AQM-52's explicit affine computations agree with AQM-50 and AQM-51: the six
`F_3` affine maps, rational residue-profile covariance, quadratic transport
`q_0 -> q_1`, reduced smearing transform, and repeated-factor dimensions check
out against the local conventions I inspected.

The main cross-shard risk is downstream citation of AQM-52 Step 6 as full
thickened Weyl covariance. AQM-51 and convention `(al)` only justify canonical
Artin-ring transport unless character transport or a momentum correction is
named.

AQM-53 is broadly aligned with convention `(am)`: ordinary points do not force
fermions; the superpoint, `Pi Omega^1` of the dual-number point, and the
two-puncture log-spin curve are deliberately catalogue entries; and the
`F_3 -> C` scalar-extension caveat is stated. The remaining risk is provenance
precision for source-backed terminology.

## Suggested Actions

   transport paragraph.
2. In AQM-53, inline exact source locators for CAR, supergeometry, spin, and log

3. In AQM-53, clarify that the `F_3` log-spin example is a local catalogue
   definition and calculation, not an analytic spin-structure theorem imported
