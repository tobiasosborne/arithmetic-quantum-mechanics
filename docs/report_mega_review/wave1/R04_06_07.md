## Findings

**Major - `report/sections/07_toric_operator_relation.tex:83-95`: the
cell-fermion "second-quantized cellular differential" is claimed to square to
zero on the Fock space without the needed graded-derivation convention.**

The operator is written as
`\sum_{e,v}(\delta^0_{\mathbb Z})_{ev}a_e^*a_v
+ \sum_{f,e}(\delta^1_{\mathbb Z})_{fe}a_f^*a_e`, then line 93 says it
preserves fermion number and line 93-95 says its square is zero because
`\delta^1_{\mathbb Z}\delta^0_{\mathbb Z}=0`. That implication is only
immediate on the one-particle sector. For the ordinary number-preserving CAR
bilinear `dGamma(D)`, `D^2=0` does not by itself imply `dGamma(D)^2=0` on
multi-particle sectors; cross terms can map two different 0-cells to two
1-cells. To make the paragraph correct, it needs either a restriction to the
one-particle sector or an explicit graded exterior-chain convention with the
Koszul signs under which the cellular differential is extended as an odd
derivation.

**Minor - `report/sections/06_toric_chain_ghost_proof.tex:68-74` and
`report/sections/07_toric_operator_relation.tex:121-128`: basis/dual
identifications are used silently.**

`06` writes `r_v=\delta^0 v` even though `\delta^0:C^0\to C^1`, and `07`
feeds a vertex `v` into `\delta^0` similarly. This is harmless if each cell is
identified with its dual basis cochain, but the identification should be stated
where first used. The local Bombin--Martin-Delgado source distinguishes cells
from dual cochains, and `CONVENTIONS.md` records the transpose convention; the
shards should make that bridge explicit to avoid a chain/cochain notation
shift.

**Minor - `report/sections/06_toric_chain_ghost_proof.tex:16-23`,
`68-85`, and `203-204`; `report/sections/07_toric_operator_relation.tex:121-143`:
the shards rely on sourced toric conventions but contain no local source or
convention pointer of their own.**

The needed anchors do exist in `CONVENTIONS.md` and in `AQM-05`, and the run
paths for the chain/ghost validation are registered in `INDEX.md` and
`data/SCHEMA.md`. Still, these two proof shards make standalone claims about
the periodic lattice, star/plaquette supports, and the shifted Hamiltonian. Add
a short pointer to `CONVENTIONS.md` entries (i)-(k), or to the same local source
locators used in `AQM-05`, so the evidence chain is visible inside the assigned
shards.

**Question - `report/sections/07_toric_operator_relation.tex:165-184`: ghost
adjoint notation may drift from the convention.**

Here exterior multiplication by `\eta_v,\theta_f` is denoted by the unstarred
ghost symbols, while annihilation is written `\gamma_i^*`. This is internally
reasonable, but `CONVENTIONS.md` writes the creation operator as `c_i^*`.
Consider adding one sentence that `\gamma_i^*` denotes the Hilbert adjoint of
the exterior-multiplication operator `\gamma_i`, not a change in the
supercharge convention.

## Cross-Shard Risks

The core distinction between `Q_{\mathrm{cell}}` and `Q_{\mathrm{gh}}` is
mostly consistent: `06:221-227` says the cellular differential supplies the
quotient set while the ghost operator selects the free complex vector space,
and `07:11-16` plus `07:227-236` says the relation is induced/cohomological,
not a low-ghost restriction.

The main cross-shard risk is the optional cell-fermion paragraph in `07:74-98`.
If left as written, it may reintroduce exactly the kind of operator-level
equivalence the shard is trying to avoid, because it asserts a full Fock-space
nilpotent supercharge without the necessary grading convention.

Run and manifest references checked for this scope appear present:
`runs/2026-05-24-toric-chain-ghost-unification/`,
`data/toric_chain_ghost_unification.csv`, `INDEX.md`, and `data/SCHEMA.md` all
refer to the chain/ghost validation. I did not validate the run by execution.
