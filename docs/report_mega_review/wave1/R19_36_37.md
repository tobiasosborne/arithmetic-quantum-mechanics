## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/37_thickened_tn_equals_1_examples.tex:5`
  Issue:
  The shard header promises explicit `t^3=1` and `t^6=1` jet commutator
  formulas, but the body only gives the explicit commutator formula for
  `n=3`.
  Evidence:
  Lines 120--143 define `q`, `p`, `T`, `R`, `W`, and the `u^2` top-coefficient
  commutator formula for `R_3 = F_3[u]/(u^3)`. The `n=6` section, lines
  147--167, gives the factorization, the two Artin factors `A_+` and `A_-`,
  and the thickened/reduced dimensions, but it never writes the product
  character or the two-factor commutator phase. This also conflicts with the
  shard catalogue summary for `AQM-37`, which repeats that both `t^3` and
  `t^6` formulas are worked explicitly.
  Suggested action:
  Either add the missing `n=6` formula, e.g. the product of the `u_+=t-1` and
  `u_-=t+1` top-coefficient phases
  `sum_{r+s=2}(p_{+,r}q'_{+,s}-p'_{+,r}q_{+,s}) +
   sum_{r+s=2}(p_{-,r}q'_{-,s}-p'_{-,r}q_{-,s})`,
  or weaken the shard header/catalogue wording to say that only the `t^3`
  commutator is displayed explicitly.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/36_nilpotent_thickening_weyl_fields.tex:220`
  Issue:
  The phrase "old residue qutrit" is too narrow for the general local theorem.
  Evidence:
  AQM-36 is stated for an arbitrary monic irreducible `pi` of degree
  `d` over `F_3` (`report/sections/36_nilpotent_thickening_weyl_fields.tex:30-33`).
  Its residue field is `K=F_3[t]/(pi)`, so the reduced local carrier has
  dimension `|K|=3^d`, not necessarily `3`. It is a qutrit only when
  `deg(pi)=1`.
  Suggested action:
  Replace "old residue qutrit" with "old residue-qudit site" or "old
  residue-field Weyl site" in AQM-36. Keep "qutrit" only in examples where the
  residue field is actually `F_3`.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/37_thickened_tn_equals_1_examples.tex:29`
  Issue:
  The phrase "one 27-dimensional third-order jet qutrit" is dimensionally
  ambiguous and can be read as calling the thickened carrier an ordinary
  qutrit.
  Evidence:
  The body correctly says that the reduced layer is one qutrit
  (`report/sections/37_thickened_tn_equals_1_examples.tex:111-118`) and that
  the thickened carrier is `ell^2(F_3[u]/(u^3))` of dimension `27`. Under
  convention `(ab)` in `CONVENTIONS.md`, a thickening attaches one thickened
  quantum site at the same closed point, not extra closed points; the local
  Hilbert space is `ell^2(A_i)`, not an ordinary `3`-level qudit.
  Suggested action:
  Rephrase E1 as "one 27-dimensional third-order jet site over the residue
  qutrit" or "one thickened site of dimension 27." This preserves the
  jets-not-points message without blurring local Hilbert dimension.

## Cross-Shard Risks

AQM-36 and AQM-37 are consistent with the main reduced-versus-thickened
dimension convention: AQM-35 gives the reduced `3^m` layer, and AQM-36/AQM-37
give the thickened `3^n` layer. The main cross-shard risk is terminology:
"qutrit" should stay reserved for actual `F_3` residue sites, while the
nilpotent-sensitive layer should be called a thickened Artin/Weyl site or jet
site.

The finite chain-ring and Frobenius assumptions in AQM-36 look locally
accounted for: the shard proves the ideal chain and the top-coefficient
character is generating for `F_3[t]/(pi^e)`, then uses the cited finite
Frobenius-ring Pauli framework for the Weyl operators. I did not find a
blocker in the L2 proof or the irreducibility/operator-basis argument.

## Suggested Actions

1. Add or de-advertise the missing `t^6` commutator formula in AQM-37.
2. Replace over-specific "qutrit" wording in AQM-36/AQM-37 with
   "residue-qudit" or "thickened jet site" where the carrier dimension is not
   exactly `3`.
