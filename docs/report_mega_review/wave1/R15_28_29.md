## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/29_spec_fq_t_arithmetic_field.tex:13`
  Issue:
  The monic-irreducible indexing of closed points, the cofinite closed support
  of nonempty opens, and the infinite-support polynomial-profile conclusion
  rely on `k[t]` being Euclidean/PID/UFD, but the shard does not cite a local
  source locator or prove that base fact.
  Evidence:
  The status anchors cite Stacks locators for spectra, `D(f)`, and residue
  fields, then say the polynomial-ring classification is "proved directly from
  the Euclidean property of `k[t]`" (`29_spec_fq_t_arithmetic_field.tex:13-19`).
  The proof begins with "Since `k[t]` is Euclidean, every nonzero ideal has a
  unique monic generator" (`29_spec_fq_t_arithmetic_field.tex:59-66`). The
  Zariski-open calculation uses the same finite factorization input
  (`29_spec_fq_t_arithmetic_field.tex:88-103`), and the regular-profile
  conclusion uses the existence of an irreducible divisor of `P+1` plus
  finiteness of the irreducible divisors of a nonzero polynomial
  (`29_spec_fq_t_arithmetic_field.tex:200-210`). This is correct standard
  algebra, but under the repository's evidence rules it needs a local source
  or an explicit local derivation. A possible local source exists in the Stacks
  snapshot, e.g. `references/algebraic_geometry/stacks_project_algebra.tex`
  around lines 8135-8138 for `K[x]` Euclidean/PID and lines 29578-29598 for
  polynomial UFD facts, but those locators are not cited or registered for this
  use in the shard.
  Suggested action:
  Add a local source anchor, or insert a short sourced lemma, for Euclidean
  division/PID/UFD/factorization in `k[t]`. Then explicitly point the closed
  point classification, the `D(g)` cofinite-support statement, and the
  polynomial-profile finite-support test to that anchor.
