## Findings

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/20_grassmann_weyl_fermion_displacements.tex:6`
  Issue: The shard keyword `projective representation` is unqualified, while the body's actual theorem is deliberately not an ordinary complex-scalar projective Hilbert representation.
  Evidence: `AQM-20` later states the representation is inside the Grassmann-extended CAR algebra (`report/sections/20_grassmann_weyl_fermion_displacements.tex:181-195`), and `AQM-21` explicitly says it is not an ordinary projective representation with complex `U(1)` multiplier (`report/sections/21_grassmann_weyl_fermion_displacement_derivations.tex:183-201`). This matches `CONVENTIONS.md:522-539`.
  Suggested action: Replace the keyword with `Grassmann-valued ray representation` or `not ordinary projective representation`; also mirror that wording in `report/SHARD_CATALOG.md` if the keyword is kept there.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/20_grassmann_weyl_fermion_displacements.tex:124-125`
  Issue: The sentence "every Grassmann monomial is nilpotent" is slightly too broad unless "positive-degree" is understood.
  Evidence: The exponent used here is a positive-degree even Grassmann element, so the finite-polynomial conclusion is correct. But as written, the statement can be read as including the degree-zero scalar monomial `1`, which is not nilpotent. The cited source supports nilpotency of Grassmann variables and finite Grassmann polynomial behavior (`references/canonical_fields/cahill_glauber_physics_9808029v1_source/fxxx.tex:603-643`), not the overbroad degree-zero phrasing.
  Suggested action: Change the sentence to "The exponential is a finite polynomial because the exponent has positive Grassmann degree and is nilpotent."

No Blocker or Major issues found in the assigned shards. The core sign checks in `AQM-21` agree with the local Cahill--Glauber source: the displacement identities match `fxxx.tex:391-414`, and the ray product sign matches `fxxx.tex:456-464`.
