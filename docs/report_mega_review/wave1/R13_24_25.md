## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:84`
  Issue:
  AQM-25 uses `\mathcal A(U)` for arbitrary quasi-compact opens
  `U \in \mathsf K(X)` by citing the open-local algebra of AQM-24, but AQM-24
  only defines `\mathcal A(S)` as a finite tensor product after explicitly
  assuming `X` is finite. For a general affine spectrum, a quasi-compact open
  need not be a finite set of finite-residue Weyl sites, so the displayed
  system may require an infinite tensor product or infinite-residue local
  Hilbert spaces that have no convention in this shard.
  Evidence:
  AQM-24 fixes the finite setting at
  `report/sections/24_zariski_opens_observable_nets.tex:70` and defines
  `\mathcal A(S)=\bigotimes_{x\in S}\mathcal A_x` at
  `report/sections/24_zariski_opens_observable_nets.tex:81`. AQM-25 then
  broadens to `X=\operatorname{Spec}R` at
  `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:54` and applies AQM-24's
  algebra to all quasi-compact opens at
  `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:84`. The later affine
  convention records the missing repair: for infinite affine schemes over
  `\mathbb F_q`, the finite qudit layer is indexed by closed points and
  finite supports, with `\mathcal A(U)` defined as a colimit over finite closed
  supports (`report/sections/28_closed_point_affine_arithmetic_fields.tex:13`
  and `report/sections/28_closed_point_affine_arithmetic_fields.tex:157`).
  Suggested action:
  Either restrict AQM-25 explicitly to finite spectra with finite residue
  fields, or replace the quasi-local system by the finite-closed-support
  construction later used in AQM-28. If the intended object is an infinite
  tensor product or a `C^*` completion over quasi-compact opens, add the
  missing convention before asserting the colimit.

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:91`
  Issue:
  The claimed "natural Zariski quasi-local algebra" over quasi-compact opens is
  degenerate for affine spectra because `X=\operatorname{Spec}R` itself is
  quasi-compact and terminal in `\mathsf K(X)`. Thus the filtered colimit is
  just `\mathcal A(X)` whenever that algebra exists. This does not behave like
  a finite-region quasi-local construction on an infinite affine space, and it
  collides with the shard's statement that no analytic completion convention is
  fixed.
  Evidence:
  AQM-25 cites `\operatorname{Spec}R` quasi-compact at
  `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:16`, defines
  `\mathsf K(X)` as quasi-compact opens at
  `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:54`, and declares
  `\mathcal A_{\rm qloc}(X)=\varinjlim_{U\in\mathsf K(X)}\mathcal A(U)` at
  `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:91`. Since `X` is an
  upper bound for every open and is itself in `\mathsf K(X)`, the directed
  system has a terminal object. The finite checks at
  `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:135` and
  `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:141` acknowledge this
  collapse in the two finite examples, but the conclusion at
  `report/sections/25_quasilocal_zariski_algebra.tex:157` states it as the
  general convention.
  Suggested action:
  Clarify whether this shard is deliberately defining the global algebra
  `\mathcal A(X)` rather than a genuine finite-region quasi-local algebra. For
  the latter, index by finite supports or by a nonterminal family of bounded
  regions, and keep quasi-compact opens as topology/control data rather than
  the region poset itself.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/24_zariski_opens_observable_nets.tex:56`
  Issue:
  The setup begins with an arbitrary commutative ring and then says only that
  `X` is finite, but the construction and conclusions use finite Weyl systems
  and finite-dimensional matrix algebras. A finite topological spectrum alone
  does not record the needed finite-residue-field hypothesis.
  Evidence:
  AQM-24 starts with "Let `R` be a commutative ring" at
  `report/sections/24_zariski_opens_observable_nets.tex:56`, assumes only
  "`X` is finite" at `report/sections/24_zariski_opens_observable_nets.tex:70`,
  and then calls the values finite-dimensional `*`-algebras at
  `report/sections/24_zariski_opens_observable_nets.tex:141`. The convention
  file's finite-affine-spectrum rule is framed around residue-qudit local
  factors and finite reduced rings, with `Spec(F_p)` and `Spec(Z/6Z)` as the
  concrete cases (`CONVENTIONS.md:580`, `CONVENTIONS.md:590`, and
  `CONVENTIONS.md:613`).
  Suggested action:
  Tighten the hypotheses in AQM-24 to "finite spectra with finite residue
  fields" or "finite rings in the residue-qudit convention." If broader rings
  are intended, add a separate infinite-residue representation convention and
  stop calling the values finite-dimensional without qualification.

## Cross-Shard Risks

AQM-24 and AQM-25 correctly separate open-local covariance from
closed-complement contravariance in the finite examples. The main risk is that
AQM-25 presents a general quasi-local convention before the later AQM-28
finite-closed-support repair is incorporated. Synthesis reviewers should check
AQM-26 through AQM-28 for whether they rely on AQM-25's quasi-compact-open
colimit literally or silently switch to finite closed supports.

The `Spec(F_p)` and `Spec(Z/6Z)` edge-case computations in AQM-24 are locally
consistent with AQM-22 and AQM-23: `Spec(F_p)` has only `\emptyset` and `X`,
and `Spec(Z/6Z)` has the two singleton standard opens with the qubit/qutrit
assignment reversed by the closed-complement convention.

## Suggested Actions

1. Restrict AQM-25 to the finite-spectrum setting of AQM-24, or rewrite it to
   use the finite-closed-support algebra of AQM-28.
2. Decide whether quasi-compact opens are intended as the actual quasi-local
   index category despite the terminal object `X`; if so, state that the
   colimit is global in affine cases.
3. Add the finite-residue-field hypothesis to AQM-24's setup before the
   finite-dimensional algebra claims.
