## Findings

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/65_quantum_system_association_vocabulary.tex:81`
  Issue:
  The evidence rule groups positive/negative comparison words together with "or explicit gap label". As written, a later shard could read this as allowing a gap label to accompany words like "agrees", "is equivalent to", or "is inequivalent to" without a completed map, invariant, or obstruction.
  Evidence:
  Lines 81-83 say every future comparison word such as "agrees", "reduces to", "is equivalent to", "is inequivalent to", or "degenerates" must cite "a local source, local derivation, test, run artifact, or explicit gap label". The next sentence at lines 84-86 correctly requires the object, structures, choices, outputs, and comparison map or invariant. The plan is stricter: `docs/quantum_system_associations/PLAN.md:30-32` says no comparison claim is finished without a concrete object, stated convention, and checkable map or invariant. `CONVENTIONS.md:2116-2129` also treats `agreement`/`inequivalence` as requiring a recorded map/invariant/obstruction, while `blocked` is only an evidence-state label.
  Suggested action:
  Split the rule into two cases: positive or negative comparison statuses require a local source/derivation/test/run plus the named map, invariant, or obstruction; explicit gap labels are allowed only for `blocked`, `unknown`, `question`, or deferred comparison rows.
