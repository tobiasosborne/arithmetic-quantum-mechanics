## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/57_derham_css_f2_entanglement_codes.tex:109`
  Issue:
  The fat-rectangle examples derive `R_Z`, stabilizer rank, Bell sectors, and
  `[[n,k,d]]` parameters from `d_1^T`, but the shard does not display a full
  `C^2` basis or `d_1` matrix/rank check for those examples. Since convention
  `(ao)` defines `R_Z=im(transpose(d1))` only after finite bases and the dot
  pairing are chosen, the report text leaves part of the evidence chain
  implicit exactly where the code-parameter claims depend on it.
  Evidence:
  `CONVENTIONS.md:1687`-`1699` says the de Rham CSS test chooses finite bases
  and forms `R_X=im(d0)`, `R_Z=im(transpose(d1))`. In the ten-qubit rectangle,
  lines 109-118 state only that `x^2\omega=0`, list four nonzero `d_1` values,
  and then assert `R_Z=<b_0+c_1,b_1,d_1>`; the `C^2` basis and independence of
  the rows \(\omega,x\omega,y\omega\) are not recorded. In the eighteen-qubit
  rectangle, lines 168-180 similarly list nonzero `d_1` rows and assert
  `R_Z=<b_0+c_1,b_1,b_2+c_3,b_3,d_1,d_3>` without recording the full target
  basis after the `x^4\omega=0` quotient or the row-rank calculation. The
  subsequent ranks and parameters at lines 124-138 and 190-202 depend on these
  row spaces.
  Suggested action:
  For each rectangle, insert the ordered `C^2` basis after the quotient
  relations, display the `d_1` matrix or row list against that basis, and state
  the row rank before deriving `R_Z`, Bell sectors, and `[[n,k,d]]`.

## Cross-Shard Risks

The displayed characteristic-two identities checked out under hand inspection:
`d(t^2)=0` and `d(y^2)=0` in characteristic two explain the extra one-form
directions, while odd powers such as `x^3` and `x^5` still impose `x^2 dx` and
`x^4 dx` relations. The selected Bell sectors in AQM-57 also match the
displayed isolated pairs of commuting `XX` and `ZZ` generators.

The main risk is that downstream readers may treat the compact `d_1` row lists
as complete matrix evidence. For the larger `F_2` examples, the report should
make the `C^2` basis and row-rank step explicit so the selected `Z`
stabilizers and `[[n,k,d]]` parameters remain auditable without rerunning or
reconstructing the calculation.

## Suggested Actions

1. Add explicit `C^2` bases and `d_1` row/rank checks for the ten- and
   eighteen-qubit fat rectangles in AQM-57.
2. Add the corresponding `C^2` basis/rank sentence for the three-variable
   square-zero example in AQM-56.
3. Inline the local Stacks source path in the AQM-56 and AQM-57 status/source
   paragraphs.
