## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/58_product_rings_real_line_cotangent.tex:159`
  Issue:
  The real-line operator-label section uses a symplectic-sign convention that
  conflicts with the newer tangent-cotangent convention it claims to answer.
  It also introduces the total cotangent bundle's "canonical algebraic
  symplectic form" without a local source or a convention fixing that sign.
  Evidence:
  AQM-58 cites convention `(ao)` at lines `10`-`18`, then writes the canonical
  form as `dp\wedge dx` and the pointwise CCR form as
  `p q'-p' q` at lines `159`-`170`. But `CONVENTIONS.md:1602`-`1606` fixes the
  geometric tangent-cotangent phase-space form as
  `omega((v,alpha),(w,beta)) = beta(v) - alpha(w)`, which in the displayed
  coordinates is `p' q - p q'`, the opposite sign. AQM-60 repeats the `(ao)`
  sign in its real-line example at
  `report/sections/60_tangent_weyl_operator_principle.tex:196`-`199`. The
  cited Stacks tangent/cotangent lines support fibres and duality, but not the
  total-cotangent-bundle symplectic-form convention or its sign.
  Suggested action:
  Choose one sign convention for the tangent-cotangent Weyl layer and make
  AQM-58, AQM-60, and `CONVENTIONS.md (ao)` agree. If AQM-58 intentionally
  imports the older AQM-19 CCR sign, state that this is the opposite of the
  `(ao)` geometric form or change the displayed form to match `(ao)`. Add a
  local source or a short local derivation for
  `T^*X = Spec Sym_A(Omega^1)` and the chosen canonical form.

- Severity: Question
  Location: `report/sections/58_product_rings_real_line_cotangent.tex:185`
  Issue:
  The statement about a "naive finite-dimensional truncation" of
  `R[x] -> R[x] dx` tending to impose all `C^1` directions is not tied to a
  defined truncation rule, so it is not presently checkable.
  Evidence:
  The exact algebraic statement just above is well supported: `d_0` is
  surjective for the full polynomial de Rham complex at lines `90`-`103`.
  But the finite de Rham CSS prototype in `CONVENTIONS.md:1608`-`1610` is
  stated for finite-dimensional vector spaces, and no convention fixes how to
  truncate `R[x]` and `R[x] dx`. Different degree cutoffs can change whether
  the truncated derivative is onto the chosen `C^1` space.
  Suggested action:
  Rephrase this as an explicitly heuristic warning, or define the finite
  truncation functor before drawing any conclusion about the induced CSS
  checks.

## Cross-Shard Risks

The main risk is symplectic sign drift between the older CCR comparison layer
and the corrected tangent-cotangent Weyl layer. AQM-58 follows the AQM-19 CCR
formula, while convention `(ao)` and AQM-60 use the opposite tangent-cotangent
ordering. This affects commutator phases and any later stabilizer-selection
claims that mix real CCR language with the finite tangent-Weyl recipe.

AQM-59's mathematical calculations for product-ring differentials, odd
finite-field circle point counts, the characteristic-two double line, and the
closed-point Mobius count formula are locally derivable from the inspected
shard text and cited Stacks/convention material. The provenance issue is the
anchor paragraph, not the point-count arithmetic itself.

## Suggested Actions

1. Reconcile the AQM-58/AQM-60/`CONVENTIONS.md (ao)` symplectic sign and source
   the total-cotangent-bundle form.
2. Patch AQM-59's source anchors to include the local Stacks path and
   convention `(ao)`.
3. Either define the real-line finite truncation rule or downgrade the
   truncation sentence to a clearly labelled heuristic.
