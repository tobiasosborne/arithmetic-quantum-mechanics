## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/85_quantum_system_association_agreement_inequivalence_table.tex:40`
  Issue: The Step 21 table imports AQM-84 proposal labels into the status-token
  vocabulary without a matching convention entry.
  Evidence: Convention `(au)` defines the QSA table tokens as `available`,
  `agreement`, `inequivalence`, `degenerate`, `zero`, `empty`, `blocked`,
  `unknown`, and `not_applicable` (`CONVENTIONS.md:2109`). AQM-84 explicitly
  says it adds no convention entry (`report/sections/84_quantum_system_association_infinite_ring_boundaries.tex:13`)
  but then defines boundary statuses such as `Generic-sector proposal` and
  `Completion proposal` (`report/sections/84_quantum_system_association_infinite_ring_boundaries.tex:43`).
  AQM-85 then says those labels are inherited by the table
  (`report/sections/85_quantum_system_association_agreement_inequivalence_table.tex:40`)
  and uses "blocked or AQM-84 proposal labels" in a status-table row
  (`report/sections/85_quantum_system_association_agreement_inequivalence_table.tex:225`).
  Suggested action: Either add these boundary labels to `CONVENTIONS.md` as
  sanctioned Step 21/22 table labels, or keep all table status cells within
  convention `(au)` and move AQM-84 categories into prose/witness columns as
  non-token classifiers.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/85_quantum_system_association_agreement_inequivalence_table.tex:222`
  Issue: The infinite-boundary snapshot conflates unavailable analytic
  completions with algebraic generic sectors that are already locally recorded
  for some examples.
  Evidence: The row groups `Spec F_q[t]`, `Spec F_q[x,y]`, and `Spec Z` and
  lists "generic sectors" under `blocked` or AQM-84 proposal labels
  (`report/sections/85_quantum_system_association_agreement_inequivalence_table.tex:225`).
  But convention `(z)` already records an algebraic rational-function layer for
  `K=F_q(t)`, including a default additive functional and an `ell^2(K)`
  representation (`CONVENTIONS.md:800`), while AQM-45 records an optional
  rational `Q`-Weyl sector with `ell^2(Q)`, character, commutator calculation,
  and local rule (`report/sections/45_spec_z_arithmetic_quantum_field.tex:161`).
  The analytic/local, adelic, profinite, `C^*`, and infinite-volume operator
  completions are correctly blocked, and the curve-generic `F_q[x,y]` cases
  still lack a convention.
  Suggested action: Split the row: mark the already recorded algebraic generic
  sectors for `F_q(t)` and `Q` as optional/available generic-fibre layers, and
  keep analytic/local, adelic, profinite, `C^*`, curve-generic, and
  infinite-product operator completions blocked or proposal-labelled.

## Cross-Shard Risks

The central downstream risk is that AQM-85's synthesis table will be read as a
normalized status-token ledger even though AQM-84 introduced additional
boundary labels outside convention `(au)`. This matters for AQM-86--AQM-87 and
the final review because "generic-sector proposal" and "completion proposal"
are useful distinctions, but they should not silently become table tokens.

The second risk is over-conservative aggregation: lumping all generic sectors
with completion blockers can hide the fact that some algebraic generic-fibre
layers are locally present while their analytic/completed variants remain
blocked.

## Suggested Actions

- Normalize AQM-84's boundary categories against `CONVENTIONS.md` before later
  tables rely on them as statuses.
- In AQM-85, separate algebraic generic-sector availability from missing
  completion conventions.
- Preserve the existing blocker discipline for finite-ring database rows,
  two-direction Artin layers, quotient-collapse examples, and analytic
  infinite-volume completions.
