## Findings

- Severity: Major
  Location: `report/sections/47_f3_affine_line_symmetry_example.tex:77`
  Issue:
  The element names in the six-element affine group table conflict with the
  stated group law and with the worked products immediately below the table.
  Evidence:
  The shard states the convention
  `g_{a,b}g_{c,d}=g_{ac,ad+b}` at line 83. With
  `tau=g_{1,1}` and `rho=g_{2,0}`, this gives
  `tau rho=g_{2,1}` and `rho tau=g_{2,2}`, exactly as lines 87--89 compute.
  But the table labels `(2,1)` as `rho tau` at line 77 and `(2,2)` as
  `rho tau^2` at line 78. Under the displayed convention, `(2,1)` is
  `tau rho` (equivalently `rho tau^2`), while `(2,2)` is `rho tau`
  (equivalently `tau^2 rho`).
  Suggested action:
  Rename the last two table rows to match the composition convention, or avoid
  word names there and use only `(a,b)`/map labels. Add one sentence fixing
  whether products are read as composition of functions right-to-left.

- Severity: Minor
  Location: `report/sections/47_f3_affine_line_symmetry_example.tex:122`
  Issue:
  The shard asserts a complete list of monic irreducible quadratics over
  `F_3`, but only explains why the three displayed polynomials are
  irreducible. It does not justify completeness.
  Evidence:
  Lines 122--126 say the monic irreducible quadratics are
  `x^2+1`, `x^2+x+2`, and `x^2+2x+2`, and then state they are irreducible
  because none has an `F_3` root. That proves membership in the list, not that
  no other monic quadratic is irreducible. The subsequent action table at
  lines 133--142 is presented as the quadratic closed-point action, so the
  completeness of the degree-two closed-point list is part of the evidence
  chain.
  Suggested action:
  Add a short local derivation, for example the count
  `(3^2-3)/2=3` for monic irreducible quadratics over `F_3`, or enumerate the
  six reducible monic quadratics by their roots/factorizations.

## Cross-Shard Risks

The main cross-shard risk is notation drift around affine-group composition.
AQM-46 fixes `g_{a,b}g_{c,d}=g_{ac,ad+b}` and AQM-47 uses the same formula, but
AQM-47's element-name table mixes the product names. Later shards that refer
to `rho tau` or `rho tau^2` by name may inherit the wrong permutation unless
they use the `(a,b)` pairs directly.

I also checked the inverse direction in the closed-point pushforward:
AQM-46's `tau_{g,pi}:K_{pi^g}->K_pi` and
`(S_g e)_{pi^g}=tau_{g,pi}^{-1}(e_pi)` are consistent with the rational-point
formula `S_g(e)_y=e_{g^{-1}y}` in AQM-47.

## Suggested Actions

Fix the `AGL_1(F_3)` table labels in AQM-47 first, because that is a concrete
wrong-name defect in the worked example. Then add the missing one-line
completeness proof for the quadratic irreducibles.
