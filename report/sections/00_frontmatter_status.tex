\section{Frontmatter, Status, and Scope}

This lab book tracks a research programme investigating possible bridges
between arithmetic zeta functions, arithmetic quantum mechanics,
supersymmetric quantum mechanics, and topological lattice models such as
Kitaev and Levin-Wen systems.

No mathematical or physical claim in this lab book is treated as established
until it is marked as \emph{Source-local} or \emph{Checked} and linked to a
local source, derivation, test, or run artifact.

\subsection{Claim Status}

The status labels are fixed in \texttt{CONVENTIONS.md}:
\emph{Question}, \emph{Proposal}, \emph{Source-local}, \emph{Checked}, and
\emph{Rejected}. These labels are part of the scientific record. They separate
programme-level analogies from results.

\subsection{Evidence Chain}

Every substantive assertion should carry the provenance required by its status:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item a \emph{Question} or \emph{Proposal} is allowed to name a gap, but must
  not be worded as an established result;
  \item a \emph{Source-local} claim must cite the relevant local source
  manifest and locator under \texttt{references/};
  \item a locally derived claim must point to the derivation and to any
  convention in \texttt{CONVENTIONS.md} on which the derivation depends;
  \item a computational or generated-data claim must point to the producer,
  run bundle, schema entry, and \texttt{INDEX.md} row; and
  \item a \emph{Checked} claim must identify the local derivation, test, or run
  artifact that checks the invariant being used.
\end{enumerate}

Missing links are allowed during exploration only when they are explicit.
